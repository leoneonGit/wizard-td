### Q: How did you recruit your testers / how do you know them?
Friends, family, and personal contacts who own Android devices and enjoy strategy games.
I shared the closed-testing opt-in link with each of them directly and confirmed they
installed and played.

### Q: How did you engage with your testers to collect feedback?
Directly, through personal messaging (WhatsApp) and in-person conversations. Testers
reported bugs, commented on difficulty balance, and flagged anything confusing in the UI;
several sent screenshots of specific issues.

### Q: What feedback did you receive, and how did you act on it?
Key feedback and the changes it drove:
- Testers found the board hard to see on phones → redesigned to a landscape-first,
  full-screen layout with clearer zoom controls and a one-time zoom/pan hint.
- Confusion about tower/relic effects → added tap-to-view info on relics and clearer
  wave-preview hints.
- Requests for audio control → added independent Sound and Music volume sliders that
  persist between sessions.
- Difficulty pacing notes → rebalanced early waves and boss encounters.

### Q: How did you decide the app is ready for production?
After 14+ days of testing with 12+ active testers across a range of Android devices, the
game was stable with no critical bugs, testers were able to play through the campaign, and
feedback was positive. The core loop, touch controls, and performance held up on every
device tested.

### Q: What is the app about? (if asked)
Wildguard TD is a roguelite 3D tower defense: the player defends a winding road across a
three-act campaign using five tower families (wizards, goblins, archers, ents, void),
drafting cards between waves and combining elemental reactions. Single-player, offline,
no ads, no in-app purchases, no data collected.
